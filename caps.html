CAPS (Counseling and Psychological Services)

Supporting Your Mental Health Journey at the University of Michigan

At the University of Michigan School of Information, we prioritize the mental health and well-being of our students through comprehensive support services offered by Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS). CAPS is dedicated to addressing the emotional, psychological, and stress-related issues that students face during their time at the university.

Services Offered

CAPS provides a range of services to support the mental health needs of our students, including:

  • Individual Therapy: Confidential sessions with licensed therapists tailored to meet the personal needs of each student.
  • Group Therapy: A variety of therapy groups are available each semester, focusing on themes such as graduate student stress, anxiety management, and building resilience.
  • Workshops and Seminars: CAPS offers workshops that provide strategies for coping with stress, enhancing well-being, and improving mental health.
  • Crisis Intervention: Immediate support is available for students experiencing severe emotional distress.

Accessing CAPS

Services at CAPS are accessible to all enrolled students. You can begin by scheduling an initial consultation through the CAPS website or by visiting the CAPS office located centrally on campus. All consultations are confidential and aimed at providing the best possible care.

Commitment to Diversity

CAPS is committed to supporting the diverse backgrounds and needs of our student body. Our staff are trained in multicultural competency and strive to provide culturally informed care across all our services. At the University of Michigan School of Information, your mental health is our priority. CAPS is here to support you every step of the way in your journey toward personal and academic success.
