Study Spaces on Campus

Discover Your Perfect Study Spot at the University of Michigan School of Information At the University of Michigan School of Information, we understand that a conducive study environment is crucial for academic success. That's why we offer a variety of study spaces designed to cater to different study needs and preferences, ensuring every student can find their ideal study spot on campus.

North Quad Spaces

Located in the heart of the School of Information, North Quad offers both quiet study areas and collaborative spaces equipped with modern technology. Whether you're working on a group project or need a silent corner to prepare for exams, North Quad has a space designed just for you. Each room is outfitted with ample power outlets, strong Wi-Fi, and comfortable seating.

The Dude (Duderstadt Center)

The James and Anne Duderstadt Center, affectionately known as "The Dude," provides a vibrant, interdisciplinary environment for creative and technological engagement. Open 24/7, it features a variety of workspaces, multimedia rooms, and access to cutting-edge technologies. Its open floor plan is perfect for students who thrive in a lively atmosphere.

Graduate Library Study Rooms

For those who prefer a more traditional setting, the Hatcher Graduate Library offers numerous quiet study rooms. These spaces are ideal for intensive study sessions and are available on a reservation basis to ensure availability.

Outdoor Spaces

Take advantage of Michigan's beautiful seasons by studying in one of the many outdoor spaces around campus. The School's courtyard, with its serene surroundings, is perfect for reading or reflective thinking.
